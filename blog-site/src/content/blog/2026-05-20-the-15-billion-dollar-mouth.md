## The Market In One Number

The Middle East cement market is worth **$15.5 billion today.** By 2029, that number grows to **$19.6 billion** at a 4.7% CAGR. This is not a niche. This is a major global consumption corridor — and it's hungry.

## The Incumbent: Turkey Owns the Table

Right now, one country dominates:

- **Turkey: $952M in cement exports** — 66% of total regional exports
- UAE: 18% (distant second)
- Iran: 6.3%

Turkey is the default. The safe choice. The option every Arab procurement officer knows.

**China's share? Statistically zero.**

That absence is not due to lack of supply. It's due to lack of a bridge.

## The Price Gap: Where Your Margin Lives

Average export price: **$66/ton**

Average import price: **$101/ton**

That's a 53% premium before you even account for freight, insurance, and spec adjustments. At the premium end, Saudi Arabia is paying **$2,261/ton** for prefabricated structural components, with import prices surging 19% YoY to $616/ton.

The spread is real. The room is there.

## The Reconstruction Signal

Post-conflict rebuilding is not theoretical — it is already in the data:

- **38 concrete factories destroyed** in Gaza during the Israel-Palestine conflict, paralyzing local output and damaging critical transport infrastructure
- Iraq's post-conflict rebuilding is creating localized demand spikes across Baghdad and Basra — roads, housing, power infrastructure
- The long-term boost from post-war reconstruction has not even begun to register in annual demand figures

This is not a future opportunity. The destruction already happened. The rebuilding is the inevitable next chapter.

## The China Angle: 70 Million Tonnes of Surplus Looking for a Home

Here is the killer insight:

Chinese cement consumption: **60 million tonnes/year**

Chinese cement production capacity: **130 million tonnes/year**

China has **more than double** the capacity it can use domestically. Its property market is stagnant. Producers are desperate for export volume.

The data backs this up:

- Chinese cement exports **rose 70%** as domestic production was cut by 25% since 2021
- That export surge still represents only **0.3%** of China's total production capacity
- Producers without strong export markets face rising bankruptcy risk

Read that again. China has the supply. The Arab world has the demand. The only missing piece is a professional, trust-based bridge between them.

## What This Means

This is not a "there might be an opportunity" pitch.

This is a **$15.5B market growing to $19.6B**, dominated by a single exporter, with a second source nation sitting on 70 million tonnes of surplus and no professional channel to market.

Turkey is feeding this mouth almost alone. China has more food than it can eat.

The bridge nobody has built yet is the entire business.
